SUMMARY OF INVESTIGATION:

On October 12, 2005, Detective Recarey conducted a follow-up interview with
Courtney Wild (DOB redacted) at the Palm Beach Police Department regarding
allegations against Jeffrey Epstein at his residence at 358 El Brillo Way,
Palm Beach, Florida.

Wild stated that between 2001 and 2004, she was recruited by Sarah Kellen
to provide "massage" services at the Epstein residence. Wild confirmed that
Ghislaine Maxwell was present at the residence during several visits and
appeared to manage the scheduling of young women.

FINANCIAL EVIDENCE:

Bank records obtained via subpoena from JPMorgan Chase reveal the following
suspicious transactions from accounts held by Jeffrey Epstein:

  - Wire transfer: $9,999.00 to account at Deutsche Bank, Zurich (March 2004)
  - Wire transfer: $9,500.00 to MC2 Model Management, Paris (April 2004)
  - Cash withdrawal: $8,000.00 from Palm Beach branch (May 2004)
  - Wire transfer: $9,900.00 to account in Cayman Islands (June 2004)
  - Cash withdrawal: $9,000.00 from Manhattan branch (July 2004)
  - Wire transfer: $50,000.00 to L Brands corporate account (August 2004)

Multiple transactions appear structured to avoid Currency Transaction Report
(CTR) filing requirements. The pattern of $9,000-$9,999 withdrawals and
wire transfers is consistent with structuring.

The FBI has been notified regarding potential Bank Secrecy Act violations.
U.S. Attorney Alexander Acosta has been briefed.

WITNESS STATEMENTS:

Annie Farmer and Maria Farmer both provided statements corroborating the
pattern of recruitment. Farmer stated that Epstein's assistant Lesley Groff
coordinated travel arrangements through Teterboro Airport.

Chief Michael Reiter has authorized continued surveillance of the El Brillo
Way property and requested assistance from the FBI and DOJ.
